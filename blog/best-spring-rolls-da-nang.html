Rolling Through Vietnam: The Story Behind the Best Spring Rolls in Da Nang

October 26, 2025 Roly Poly Fresh Spring Rolls & Coffee Da Nang

Spring rolls are a delicious snapshot of Vietnamese cooking. They are simple, fresh, and full of balance. In Da Nang you can try three classic styles: Gỏi Cuốn, Ram Cuốn Cải, and Bánh Tráng Cuốn Thịt Heo. Below you’ll find what each one is, where to eat it, a photo, and a Google Map.

1) Gỏi Cuốn — Fresh Salad Rolls

Soft rice paper wraps lettuce, herbs, vermicelli noodles, shrimp, and pork. The rolls are served fresh (not fried) with peanut–hoisin or nước chấm (fish sauce). In Da Nang, they taste especially bright thanks to local herbs and coastal shrimp.

Where to try it: Roly Poly Fresh Spring Rolls & Coffee Da Nang
Address: 05 An Thượng 6, Ngũ Hành Sơn, Đà Nẵng 550000
Hours: Mon–Sat, 10:00–20:00 (Closed Sun)

2) Ram Cuốn Cải — Crispy Rolls with Mustard Greens

These bite-size rolls are fried until golden and crunchy, then wrapped at the table with mustard greens, herbs, and rice paper. Dip in sweet–spicy nước chấm for the best flavor contrast: cool leaves outside, hot crispy roll inside.

Where to try it: Ram Cuốn Cải Việt
Address: Opposite 337 Hải Phòng, Chính Gián, Hải Châu, Đà Nẵng
Hours: 17:00–22:00 daily

3) Bánh Tráng Cuốn Thịt Heo — Da Nang’s Pork Rolls

This DIY set comes with thinly sliced boiled pork belly, herbs and vegetables, and soft rice paper. Roll it yourself and dip in mắm nêm (fermented anchovy sauce) for a bold, savory finish.

Where to try it: Donald Trung — Bánh Tráng Cuốn Thịt Heo
Address: 141 Đỗ Bá, Bắc Mỹ Phú, Ngũ Hành Sơn, Đà Nẵng 550000
Phone: +84 901 875 875
Hours: 07:00–22:00 daily

Why Spring Rolls Matter in Vietnam

All three styles share the same idea: balance. You get freshness from herbs, texture from rice paper or crispy wrappers, and a bright dip to bring everything together. It’s simple food, made with care.
