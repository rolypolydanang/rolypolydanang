From Peanut to Sesame: The Story Behind Our House-Made Dipping Sauces

August 15, 2025

At Roly Poly Fresh Spring Rolls Da Nang, our gỏi cuốn — fresh Vietnamese rice paper rolls, also called summer rolls — are the stars of the menu. But if you ask us, the dipping sauces deserve just as much attention. In Vietnamese cuisine, a sauce isn’t just an afterthought — it’s what ties the dish together, bringing balance and depth to every bite.

We make our sauces fresh every day, using high-quality, locally sourced ingredients. Whether you’re vegan, gluten-free, or just a flavor enthusiast, there’s a sauce for you. Here’s the story behind our three house-made favorites.

Peanut Sauce: Creamy, Nutty, Comforting

Our peanut sauce is rich, smooth, and gently sweet, with a roasted aroma that makes it hard to resist. It’s completely vegan, made without fish sauce or dairy, and pairs beautifully with rolls like:

  • Tofu, Mushroom & Avocado — the creamy avocado and umami mushrooms match the nutty richness.
  • Poached Chicken & Avocado — the peanut sauce adds a warm, savory depth.

Peanut sauce is often the first dipping sauce travelers fall in love with when they try Vietnamese rice paper rolls, and it’s easy to see why — it turns every bite into a comfort-food moment.

Toasted Sesame Sauce: Rich, Aromatic, Vegan

Our toasted sesame sauce is another vegan favorite. Made from freshly toasted sesame seeds blended into a smooth, earthy dressing, it has a natural sweetness and a deep, nutty flavor. We recommend it with:

  • Smoked Goose — the smoky meat and nutty sesame are a perfect match.
  • Tofu, Mushroom & Avocado — the sauce adds complexity without overpowering the fresh greens.

This sauce is always mild — we never add chili to sesame sauce — making it perfect for those who prefer gentle, earthy flavors.

Nước Chấm: The Quintessential Vietnamese Dipping Sauce

Nước chấm is a cornerstone of Vietnamese cuisine. Our version is gluten-free and made with fresh lime juice, garlic, chili, and a touch of sweetness to balance the savory notes of fish sauce. Light, tangy, and refreshing, it works beautifully with:

  • Garlic Prawn — the acidity brightens the seafood’s natural sweetness.
  • Chicken Satay — adds a fresh counterpoint to the grilled spices.

We can serve nước chấm without chili by default — and add fresh chili on request for those who want more heat.

Why Sauces Matter

In Vietnamese cooking, balance is everything — sweet, sour, salty, spicy, and umami all have their place. A dipping sauce is where these elements come together, enhancing the flavors of the rice paper roll rather than masking them. It’s why we never serve bottled sauces — freshness is key.

Pairing Tips for First-Timers

  • For seafood rolls — start with nước chấm for brightness.
  • For vegan rolls — try both peanut and sesame for a flavor contrast.
  • For meat rolls — sesame pairs well with smoked meats; peanut works with grilled flavors.

FAQ

Are your peanut and sesame sauces gluten-free?

No — they contain soy sauce, which has gluten. Only our nước chấm is gluten-free.

Can I buy your sauces to take home?

We’re working on offering bottled versions — ask our staff for details.

Are the sauces spicy?

No — none of our sauces are spicy by default. We can add fresh chili to peanut sauce or nước chấm upon request, but our sesame sauce is always mild.

At Roly Poly Fresh Spring Rolls Da Nang, we believe the right dipping sauce can turn a good roll into an unforgettable one. Whether you’re enjoying fresh spring rolls, rice paper rolls, or summer rolls, our house-made sauces will take your meal to the next level.
